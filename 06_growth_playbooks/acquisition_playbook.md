## Acquisition Philosophy

Dorje Teas should acquire customers by matching intent to product context, not by buying broad traffic. Premium Darjeeling tea needs trust before conversion: estate provenance, flush specificity, brewing confidence, and contribution margin discipline matter more than raw sessions.

The acquisition question is not "how much traffic did we buy?" The question is:

> Which traffic produces contribution-margin-positive repeat D2C revenue?

## Why Intent Beats Volume

High-volume audiences can dilute conversion if they do not understand Darjeeling quality, seasonality, or price. High-intent searches such as "First Flush Darjeeling" or "premium tea gift India" should be valued above broad tea traffic because they arrive with a clearer job to be done.

## Trust Before Conversion

Before a visitor buys First Flush Darjeeling or Second Flush Darjeeling, the page must answer:

- Why this estate?
- Why this flush?
- Why this price?
- Why buy direct instead of marketplace?
- What happens after the first purchase?

If these questions are unanswered, CAC rises and contribution margin weakens.

## Budget Allocation Logic

| Contribution ROAS Pattern | Budget Action |
|---|---|
| >= 1.5 and CVR stable | Increase spend gradually, no more than 20% per week [SYNTHETIC] |
| 1.0 to 1.5 | Hold spend; test landing page or creative before scaling |
| 0.8 to 1.0 with strong CTR | Fix landing page, offer, or product-page proof |
| < 0.8 | Pause spend until economics are repaired |

ROAS should never be used alone. A campaign with acceptable ROAS and weak contribution margin is not a scale candidate.

## Decision Table

| Metric Pattern | Interpretation | Action |
|---|---|---|
| CTR strong, CVR weak | Message earns click but page does not convert | Fix landing page proof, price framing, or product fit |
| CVR strong, contribution ROAS weak | Variable costs or spend too high | Review discount, shipping, packaging, and CAC |
| High gift AOV, low gift CM | Packaging or shipping absorbing margin | Adjust gift bundle economics before scaling |
| First Flush campaign strong in March-April | Seasonal demand is active | Scale carefully while stock and CM hold |
| Original Darjeeling Chai CAC high but repeat rate strong | First order may need repeat to pay back | Hold spend and deepen Day 21/30 retention |
| Meta prospecting CTR low and CVR low | Weak audience-message fit | Pause or rebuild audience and creative |
