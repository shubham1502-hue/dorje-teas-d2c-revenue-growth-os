## Why Subscription Timing Matters

Tea Club should not be pushed as a first-visit shortcut. Premium Darjeeling subscription works after the buyer trusts taste, freshness, delivery, and replenishment cadence.

The operating rule:

> Earn the second purchase before asking for the subscription.

## Churn Reason Taxonomy

| Reason | Signal | Operator Response |
|---|---|---|
| Over-supply | Customer has too much tea | Offer pause or lower frequency |
| Product fatigue | Same SKU every month | Offer rotation or seasonal curation |
| Price | Cancel after price reminder | Test value framing before discount |
| Delivery | Late or damaged shipment | Fix logistics and send recovery note |
| Forgot | Surprise renewal complaint | Improve pre-renewal nudge |
| Wrong cadence | Customer wants quarterly not monthly | Offer monthly to quarterly switch |

## Subscription Lifecycle Flows

| Flow | Timing | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-renewal nudge | 5 days before renewal | Reduce surprise and remind value |
| Renewal confirmation | Renewal day | Confirm shipment and set expectation |
| Pause flow | Cancel intent | Save customers with too much tea |
| Churn survey | Cancel completion | Identify true churn driver |
| Reactivation after pause | 30 days before pause ends | Restart only when timing is right |

## Decision Rules

| Pattern | Decision |
|---|---|
| Attach rate low, repeat rate strong | Fix subscription offer or timing |
| First renewal weak | Diagnose onboarding, cadence, and product fit |
| Churn due to over-supply | Add pause and quarterly cadence |
| Subscription CM below one-time CM | Review discount, packaging, and shipping cost |
| Subscriber LTV:CAC above 3x and churn controlled | Scale retention acquisition into subscription-ready cohorts |
