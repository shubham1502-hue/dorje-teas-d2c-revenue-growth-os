## 3. Acquisition Performance

Google Search delivered the best sample efficiency during First Flush demand. Meta Prospecting produced useful audience signal but weaker contribution ROAS. Email and direct traffic carried the strongest contribution margin because they used existing trust.

Decision implication: in May, hold broad Meta spend and move testing toward First Flush Darjeeling buyer retention.

## 4. Conversion Performance

First Flush Darjeeling product CVR improved to 3.5% [SYNTHETIC]. Darjeeling Green Tea CVR lagged at 1.9% [SYNTHETIC], likely because Health/Wellness Consumer traffic needs benefit-led framing before estate narrative.

Decision implication: build a Health/Wellness Consumer landing page for Darjeeling Green Tea and Cold Brew Darjeeling.

## 5. Retention and Cohort Update

The April 2025 cohort had the highest new customer count in the sample data. M1 retention is modeled at 28.1% [SYNTHETIC], stronger than the annual average. The October 2024 gifting cohort still has the highest M0 AOV [SYNTHETIC], but repeat behavior should be judged on self-purchase conversion, not same-product replenishment.

Decision implication: segment April buyers by First Flush Darjeeling, Original Darjeeling Chai, and gift order status before sending follow-up.

## 6. Subscription Update

Tea Club attach after second purchase is modeled at 13% for the April cohort [SYNTHETIC]. Subscription economics are stronger than one-time paid acquisition because renewal orders carry lower attributed media cost.

Decision implication: keep Tea Club prompt after second purchase or high engagement, not as the default first-purchase option.
