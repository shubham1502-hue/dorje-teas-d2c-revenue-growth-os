# Landing Page Experiment Backlog - Dorje Teas D2C Revenue Growth OS

> Public-information based experiment backlog. No internal Dorje test data used.
> Minimum sample sizes and targets are [SYNTHETIC] planning assumptions.

### Experiment 1: Price-Per-Cup Framing Near Product Price
**Hypothesis:** Showing price per cup near the price will reduce premium-price friction for Original Darjeeling Chai and First Flush Darjeeling.
**Primary metric:** Add-to-cart rate.
**Secondary metric:** Contribution margin per order.
**Segment:** Daily Premium Tea Drinker.
**Control:** Product page with pack price only.
**Variant:** Product page shows price per cup beside pack price.
**Minimum run time:** 14 days.
**Minimum sample size:** 1,000 product page sessions [SYNTHETIC].
**Decision rule:** Scale if ATC improves by 10% and CM% does not fall.
**Risk:** Price-per-cup framing may distract Darjeeling Loyalist buyers from harvest quality.

### Experiment 2: Estate Proof Above Product Description
**Hypothesis:** Moving Selim Hill estate proof above description will increase trust for First Flush Darjeeling buyers.
**Primary metric:** Product page CVR.
**Secondary metric:** Time on page.
**Segment:** Darjeeling Loyalist.
**Control:** Estate proof below product copy.
**Variant:** Estate proof, harvest, and certification visible above description.
**Minimum run time:** 14 days.
**Minimum sample size:** 800 product page sessions [SYNTHETIC].
**Decision rule:** Scale if CVR improves by 8% and bounce rate does not rise.
**Risk:** Too much proof above the fold may crowd out product clarity.

### Experiment 3: First Flush Landing Page vs Generic Product Page
**Hypothesis:** A dedicated First Flush Darjeeling seasonal page will convert better than a generic product page during March-April.
**Primary metric:** CVR.
**Secondary metric:** Contribution ROAS.
**Segment:** Darjeeling Loyalist and Climate/Story-Led Buyer.
**Control:** Generic First Flush Darjeeling product page.
**Variant:** Seasonal page with harvest story, tasting notes, and batch framing.
**Minimum run time:** 21 days.
**Minimum sample size:** 1,500 sessions [SYNTHETIC].
**Decision rule:** Scale if CVR and contribution ROAS both improve.
**Risk:** Seasonal page may not work outside First Flush window.

### Experiment 4: Subscription Default vs One-Time Default
**Hypothesis:** Defaulting to subscription too early will reduce first-purchase CVR, even if subscription attach rises.
**Primary metric:** First-purchase CVR.
**Secondary metric:** Subscription attach rate after second purchase.
**Segment:** Daily Premium Tea Drinker.
**Control:** One-time purchase default.
**Variant:** Subscription option selected by default.
**Minimum run time:** 14 days.
**Minimum sample size:** 1,000 sessions [SYNTHETIC].
**Decision rule:** Keep one-time default unless subscription lift offsets CVR loss through CM-positive LTV.
**Risk:** Can create early churn if subscription is pushed before trust.
