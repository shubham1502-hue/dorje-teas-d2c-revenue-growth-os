# Customer Segments and Use Cases  -  Dorje Teas

> Segment-level operating logic for acquisition, conversion, retention, and revenue review.
> Based on public brand positioning and category analysis. No internal Dorje customer data used.

---

## Why Segments Matter for a Premium Tea Brand

Dorje sells to buyers with fundamentally different triggers, purchase frequencies, and lifecycle behaviors. A retention flow built for the Daily Premium Tea Drinker will fail the Gift Buyer. A landing page built for the Darjeeling Loyalist will under-convert the Health/Wellness Consumer. Treating all traffic as one segment produces average results across all of them.

The operating system should track, acquire, and retain each segment differently.

---

## Segment Map

### Segment 1  -  The Daily Premium Tea Drinker

**Who they are:**
Urban, educated, 28–45, drinks tea daily, has moved away from commodity tea bags, is willing to pay more for quality but needs habitual replenishment, not seasonal discovery.

**Why they choose Dorje:**
- Whole leaf quality for everyday consumption
- Chai and First Flush / Second Flush as daily ritual products
- Consistent taste, no blending uncertainty

**Primary products:**
- Original Darjeeling Chai
- Pyramid Teabags (convenience format for daily use)
- Second Flush (muscatel, complex but repeatable)

**Acquisition channel:**
- Search intent ("buy Darjeeling tea online," "premium loose leaf tea India")
- Repeat organic or direct traffic after first purchase
- WhatsApp or email replenishment reminders

**Conversion trigger:**
- Price-per-cup framing (makes premium pricing rational for daily use)
- Consistent quality proof (reviews, social proof, "1,00,000 customers" signal)
- Brewing guide (reduces anxiety about switching from tea bags)

**Retention mechanism:**
- Replenishment reminder at Day 21 (100g pack cycle)
- Subscription prompt after second order
- Cross-sell to Second Flush or Green Tea after Chai repeat

**Key metrics:**
- Repeat purchase rate at Day 30 and Day 60
- Time to second purchase
- Subscription attach rate
- Replenishment cycle completion rate

**Risk:**
Price increase or out-of-stock event breaks the habit. This segment is loyal to routine, not necessarily to brand.

### Segment 2  -  The Darjeeling Loyalist

**Who they are:**
40–60, Bengali or Himalayan-region connection, childhood memory of Darjeeling tea, very specific about origin, flush, and estate, compares Dorje to what their family has always bought from physical Darjeeling sources, may have visited Darjeeling personally.

**Why they choose Dorje:**
- Selim Hill estate is specific, verifiable, and traceable
- Organic certification aligns with their quality bar
- Whole leaf, direct from estate  -  removes the doubt about blending or stale stock

**Primary products:**
- First Flush (seasonal, highest priority for this segment)
- Second Flush (muscatel character  -  this segment knows what muscatel means)
- Green Tea (Darjeeling green, not generic green tea)

**Acquisition channel:**
- Search intent ("Selim Hill tea," "First Flush Darjeeling," "estate grown Darjeeling tea online")
- Word of mouth  -  this segment talks about tea
- Content: harvest updates, estate stories, flush notes

**Conversion trigger:**
- Estate provenance proof (Selim Hill, 1871, location, altitude, certification)
- Flush specificity (First Flush Darjeeling, picking date, tasting notes)
- No blending, no middlemen  -  direct from garden
- Founder or estate story

**Retention mechanism:**
- First Flush pre-order or early access
- Harvest update emails before flush availability
- Second Flush follow-up after First Flush purchase
- Tea Club as a curated seasonal curation

**Key metrics:**
- First Flush repeat purchase rate year-over-year
- AOV (this segment buys more, not just once)
- Tea Club attach rate
- Seasonal campaign CVR

**Risk:**
If First Flush sells out or quality is inconsistent, this segment leaves loudly. Logistics and freshness promise must be kept.

### Segment 3  -  The Gift Buyer

**Who they are:**
25–50, purchasing for someone else  -  festival gifting, corporate gifting, thank-you gifts, wedding favors, housewarming. May not drink premium tea themselves. Prioritizes presentation, story, and the experience of opening the gift.

**Why they choose Dorje:**
- Premium story makes the gift feel meaningful, not generic
- "Estate-grown Darjeeling" is a story they can tell the recipient
- Alt Carbon and revival narrative gives the gift social meaning
- Selim Hill Gift Box Classic or Selim Hill Gift Box Premium is ready to give without extra packaging effort

**Primary products:**
- Selim Hill Gift Box Classic
- Selim Hill Gift Box Premium
- First Flush / Second Flush in gift format
- Pyramid Teabags as gifting add-on (accessible, convenient)
- Premium bundle with brewing guide or accessories

**Acquisition channel:**
- Search intent ("premium tea gift India," "Darjeeling tea gift box," "corporate tea gifting")
- Google Shopping
- Festive campaign: Diwali, Christmas, New Year, wedding season
- Corporate gifting outreach or inquiry path

**Conversion trigger:**
- Packaging presentation proof (photos of unboxing, gift box visual)
- Story the giver can tell ("sourced from a 150-year-old estate in Darjeeling")
- Delivery promise and reliability
- AOV-appropriate pricing (Gift Buyer customers expect to spend more)
- Personalisation option if available

**Retention mechanism (different from other segments):**
- Post-gift self-purchase prompt: "you gifted Dorje  -  here is the same for yourself"
- Gift receiver introduction email (if email capture exists in gifting flow)
- Next gifting window reminder (Diwali → Christmas → Next Diwali)

**Key metrics:**
- Gift box CVR and AOV
- Gift-buyer-to-self-purchaser conversion rate
- Corporate gifting inquiry volume and close rate
- Gifting repeat rate (does this buyer gift again next season?)
- Contribution margin after premium packaging cost

**Risk:**
Gift Buyer customers acquired through heavy seasonal discounting will not return at full price. Gift boxes should be positioned as premium, not promoted as deals.

### Segment 4  -  The Health/Wellness Consumer

**Who they are:**
25–40, urban, fitness-adjacent, researching "healthier alternatives to coffee," "antioxidant tea," "caffeine without crash," "clean ingredients." May not know Darjeeling specifically. Attracted to the organic certification, clean sourcing, and natural product story.

**Why they choose Dorje:**
- Organic certification reduces ingredient anxiety
- Whole leaf is perceived as cleaner than mass-market tea bags
- Green Tea, Cold Brew, and wellness range fit their consumption format
- No additives, no blending uncertainty

**Primary products:**
- Darjeeling Green Tea
- Darjeeling Green Tea and Cold Brew Darjeeling
- Cold Brew (modern, low-friction format)
- Pyramid Teabags (clean, convenient)

**Acquisition channel:**
- Search intent ("organic green tea India," "Darjeeling green tea benefits," "clean tea online")
- Instagram or YouTube content on tea rituals, wellness routines
- Health and wellness creator partnerships
- Google Shopping for green tea or wellness tea

**Conversion trigger:**
- Organic certification proof (visible and early on the product page)
- Health benefit framing (antioxidants, caffeine profile, clean sourcing)
- Format flexibility (Cold Brew, Pyramid Teabags for on-the-go use)
- Price-per-serving framing (vs. coffee or other wellness alternatives)

**Retention mechanism:**
- Wellness education: how Green Tea from Darjeeling differs from mass-market green tea
- Cold Brew replenishment cycle
- Cross-sell to Chai as a transition product from coffee

**Key metrics:**
- Green Tea and Cold Brew CVR
- New customer acquisition cost via wellness-adjacent channels
- Repeat purchase rate at Day 45 (wellness routines take time to form)
- Cross-sell rate from Green Tea to Chai

**Risk:**
This segment is the most price-sensitive and the most easily poached by competing health tea brands. Organic and wellness claims must be backed by concrete product page content, not just labels.

### Segment 5  -  The Climate/Story-Led Buyer

**Who they are:**
28–45, educated, urban, values-aligned consumer. Reads about sustainability, conscious consumption, and regenerative economics. Connected to the Alt Carbon and Darjeeling revival narrative. This is not a mass segment  -  it is a loyalty-and-advocacy segment.

**Why they choose Dorje:**
- Selim Hill Collective: community welfare, fair wages, organic since early 2000s, Rainforest certification
- Alt Carbon's Darjeeling revival thesis: soil health, carbon removal, land and community
- Buying Dorje is a values-consistent purchase, not just a tea purchase
- They want to tell others about it

**Primary products:**
- First Flush (highest origin provenance)
- Tea Club subscription (ongoing connection to the estate)
- Gift boxes (gifting as values expression)

**Acquisition channel:**
- Content: estate stories, harvest updates, community and soil narratives
- Newsletter or email  -  this segment reads
- Podcast or long-form content featuring Alt Carbon, Darjeeling, or regenerative agriculture
- Word of mouth  -  this is Dorje's most likely referral segment

**Conversion trigger:**
- Selim Hill story (history, altitude, community, certification)
- Impact narrative (buying this tea supports what Alt Carbon is doing in Darjeeling)
- Transparency: who grows it, how it's grown, what the estate is doing

**Retention mechanism:**
- Harvest update before each flush
- Estate news or Alt Carbon updates via email
- Tea Club as an ongoing "subscription to the estate"
- Community content: brewing rituals, estate visits, behind-the-scenes

**Key metrics:**
- Email subscriber open rate (this segment over-indexes on email engagement)
- Tea Club attach rate
- Referral rate or word-of-mouth acquisition
- Content-assisted conversion rate
- Subscription renewal rate

**Risk:**
If the narrative is vague or feels like marketing copy rather than genuine estate practice, this segment disengages. Authenticity is the product for this buyer.

## Cross-Segment Operating Rules

1. **Segment all post-purchase flows.** A Gift Buyer receiving a daily replenishment nudge is a waste and a friction event.
2. **Do not push subscription before segment-appropriate trust is built.** Darjeeling Loyalist and Climate/Story-Led Buyer segments will subscribe. Daily Premium Tea Drinker segments will subscribe after the second purchase. Gift Buyer segments rarely subscribe  -  follow up on self-purchase first.
3. **First Flush launch should prioritize Darjeeling Loyalist and Climate/Story-Led Buyer segments.** These two segments have the highest first-flush purchase intent and the highest post-purchase advocacy.
4. **Health/Wellness Consumer campaigns need separate landing pages.** A wellness search landing on the Dorje homepage with estate origin above the fold will under-convert. The Health/Wellness Consumer needs benefits, format, and clean sourcing above the fold.
5. **Gift Buyer AOV should not be compared against Daily Premium Tea Drinker AOV.** Average blended AOV hides the real economics of each segment.
