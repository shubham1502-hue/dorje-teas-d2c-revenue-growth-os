# Founder Decision Log Template

Use this log to separate founder-level decisions from operator actions.

Every decision should have evidence, a follow-up date, an actual result, and a learning. If no one reviews the result, the decision log becomes meeting notes instead of an operating system.

## Decision Log

| Decision date | Decision owner | Business question | Options considered | Evidence used | Decision | Expected impact | Risk | Follow-up date | Actual result | Learning |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Date] | [Founder / role] | [What decision must be made?] | [Option A, Option B, Option C] | [Metrics, customer signal, margin data, public-safe assumption, or operator read] | [Chosen option] | [What should change?] | [What could go wrong?] | [Date] | [Result after review] | [What this teaches us] |

## What Counts As A Founder Decision

Use this log for decisions involving:

- budget allocation
- pricing or discount policy
- product priority
- channel scale or pause
- margin tradeoffs
- brand risk
- timing tradeoffs
- hiring or ownership changes
- major experiment prioritization

Do not use this log for routine operator tasks such as changing a subject line, updating a dashboard filter, or fixing a broken export.

## Evidence Quality Bar

Good evidence is specific and decision-relevant:

- contribution margin by channel
- funnel stage movement
- repeat purchase or cohort signal
- subscription renewal or churn signal
- product-level margin
- customer segment behavior
- inventory or fulfillment constraint
- clearly labeled assumption when real data is not available

Weak evidence should be labeled as weak:

- one-week movement without context
- blended ROAS without margin
- sample data
- synthetic data
- public observation
- anecdotal customer feedback
